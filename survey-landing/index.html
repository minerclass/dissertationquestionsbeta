Study Overview

Generative AI, learning, and the conditions that shape meaningful schoolwork

This study examines how K-12 educators are navigating generative AI tools such as ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Copilot, MagicSchool, and similar tools. The survey asks about student thinking, assessment design, classroom practice, school leadership, district policy, and the conditions that help educators preserve meaningful learning in the age of AI.

Participation is voluntary. The landing page does not collect survey answers, names, emails, or device information.

Framework

A short overview of the Pedagogical Friction Framework

Noetic Friction

Protecting the cognitive struggle students need to generate, attempt, revise, and explain their thinking.

Rhetorical Friction

Preserving opportunities for students to test ideas with real audiences through dialogue, critique, presentation, and defense of learning.

Existential Friction

Supporting student ownership, authorial voice, uncertainty, personal judgment, and meaningful connection to the work.

Infrastructural Friction

Examining the policies, leadership conditions, assessment systems, professional learning, and governance structures that shape AI use.

Productive vs. Exclusionary Friction

Distinguishing difficulty that supports learning from barriers that unnecessarily block access, equity, or participation.

Study Overview

Generative AI, learning, and the conditions that shape meaningful schoolwork

This study examines how K-12 educators are navigating generative AI tools such as ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Copilot, MagicSchool, and similar tools. The survey asks about student thinking, assessment design, classroom practice, school leadership, district policy, and the conditions that help educators preserve meaningful learning in the age of AI.

Participation is voluntary. The landing page does not collect survey answers, names, emails, or device information.

Study Topics

What the survey asks about

Teaching and Learning

How generative AI is affecting classroom practice, assignments, assessment, and student learning.

Professional Experience

What educators and leaders are observing, trying, questioning, and needing in their professional settings.

Policy and Support

How policies, professional learning, technology access, and organizational conditions shape AI use.

Equity and Access

How generative AI may create supports, barriers, or different experiences across educational communities.

FAQ

Common participant questions

Do I need to be an AI expert?

No. The survey is intended for educators with a range of experience, including those who are new to generative AI.

What if I fit more than one role?

Use the single survey link. The survey form will ask you to identify the role or perspective that best fits your professional work.

Will the website collect my answers?

No. This website only provides study information and links to the official survey form.

Should I name my school, district, students, or colleagues?

No. Please avoid including names or identifiable information in open-ended responses.

Can I share this survey with other educators?

Yes, once the study is approved for distribution and the official survey links are active.

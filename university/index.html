Consent and Context

This page collects adult university student responses for a draft dissertation instrument. Use only after final protocol review and IRB approval.

Research caution: students may skip any question. Avoid collecting school names, course names, instructor names, or other direct identifiers unless approved.
All response data remains local until export.

Brief Definitions

Use these short definitions for consistency before the student begins the instrument.

Cognitive struggle: the effort of organizing, testing, revising, and making sense of difficult ideas.
Authorship: the student's sense of ownership and responsibility for the academic claim or product.
Productive scaffold: support that removes an unnecessary barrier while keeping the student responsible for understanding.
Short-circuiting: AI solves or smooths the task before the student has processed the idea deeply enough.

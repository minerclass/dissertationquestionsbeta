Methodological Stance

For proposal and committee review, the study should be described as a qualitative-dominant convergent mixed methods design grounded in constructivist qualitative inquiry. Interviews and other open-ended evidence provide the qualitative core. Closed-ended survey items and national datasets provide supporting quantitative context. Adult university students are a participant group, and AI-generated texts are analyzed as a distinct nonparticipant comparison source.

Recommended Data Workflow

  1. Participant completes the appropriate role-specific instrument.
  2. Participant downloads a .json or .md export.
  3. Audio, if used, is downloaded separately and handled through the approved process.
  4. Exports are transferred through the IRB-approved secure workflow, not through this public site.
  5. You review or analyze files locally or in a protected research storage environment.

Cognitive Interview Note (Component 1): When using these tools to validate the survey, instruct the participant to 'think aloud' as they complete the quick items, reflection prompts, and card sort (whichever the instrument includes). Use the retrospective follow-up textboxes to record notes on comprehension difficulties, ambiguity in response options, or terminology problems.

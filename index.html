Consent and Local Data Notice

This tool supports adult participant intake and reflective response collection. It is a draft research instrument and should be used only after IRB approval and final committee review.

IRB note: audio recording is optional and browser-local. The researcher should still describe recording, retention, export, deletion, and transmission procedures in consent materials.
Avoid names, school names, email addresses, or other direct identifiers when possible.
Nothing is submitted online from this page.

Brief Definitions

Use a short, consistent frame before data collection. This avoids over-priming while giving participants enough language to answer thoughtfully.

Productive friction: effort, ambiguity, revision, or struggle that supports deeper learning.
Exclusionary friction: barriers that block access, dignity, communication, or fair participation.
Authorship: the participant's sense of ownership and responsibility for an academic claim or product.
AI assistance: generative tools used for brainstorming, explanation, drafting, editing, summarizing, translation, coding, or feedback.

Quick Response Items

These selected-response and short-answer items create structured indicators for later comparison with qualitative reflections.

Typed or Recorded Reflection

Participants may type responses, record audio locally, or do both. If recording, add or edit a transcript/summary before export.

Card Sort

Sort each activity by the amount of productive cognitive friction it preserves and whether it is a legitimate AI use in education.

Review and Export

Review the structured export before downloading. The export includes role, framework tags, selected responses, reflections, follow-up notes, and card-sort placements.

Export preview

Participant exports the file and chooses how to share it with the researcher.
